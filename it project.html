Aswan

Look at the beauty of its places

Philae Temple, dedicated to the goddess Isis, is located on an island in the Nile River near Aswan. This ancient temple was relocated from its original location due to the construction of the Aswan High Dam. With its picturesque setting and well-preserved hieroglyphs, Philae Temple is a must-visit attraction for history enthusiasts and tourists alike.

The Aswan High Dam is a marvel of modern engineering located on the Nile River in Aswan, Egypt. Built in the 1960s, the dam controls flooding, provides water for irrigation, and generates hydroelectric power. Its construction led to the creation of Lake Nasser, one of the largest artificial lakes in the world. Visitors can learn about the dam's significance and impact at the nearby Aswan High Dam Visitor Center.

The Abu Simbel Temples are two massive rock temples located in Abu Simbel, near the border with Sudan. Built during the reign of Pharaoh Ramesses II in the 13th century BCE, these temples are dedicated to the gods Amun, Ra-Horakhty, and Ptah, as well as to the deified Pharaoh himself. The temples were relocated in the 1960s to avoid being submerged by the waters of Lake Nasser after the construction of the Aswan High Dam.

The Aswan Botanical Gardens, located on Kitchener's Island in the Nile River, offer a tranquil escape from the bustling city. Established by Lord Kitchener in the early 20th century, the gardens boast a diverse collection of plant species from around the world. Visitors can explore lush greenery, colorful flowers, and scenic views of the Nile while enjoying a peaceful respite from urban life.
